Objective

The object of the game is to form rows of five poker chips on the board by placing the chips on the board spaces corresponding to cards played from your hand.

Directions

Players

The game can be played with two to four players. For two or three players, they each is a team (so the game with three players will have three teams). Four players will divide into teams evenly. In the game page, a colored bar besides the avatar shows the team of the player.

Board and Cards

The game uses two full standard card decks (52 cards each, 104 cards total, no Jokers.) Each card is pictured twice on the game board, and Jacks do not appear on the board.

Players each has five cards in the game.

In Game

The players take turn to act. In your turn, choose a card from your hand, and click on one of the corresponding spaces of the game board (Example: you choose Ace of Diamonds from your hand and click on the Ace of Diamonds on the board). A token will appear on that space and ends your turn. You will automatically draw a new card from deck pile.

Jacks are wild. Two-Eyed Jacks (clubs and diamonds) can represent any card and may be used to place a counter on any space on the board. One-Eyed Jacks (spades and hearts) can remove an opponent’s token from a space. Players may use the Two-Eyed Jacks to complete a row or block an opponent, and One-Eyed Jacks can remove an opponent’s advantage. However, One-Eyed Jacks cannot be used to remove a marker chip that is already part of a completed sequence. Once a sequence is achieved by a player or team, it stands. Once a Jack is played, it ends the turn.

The played card then goes face-up into a “Discard” pile, and play passes to the next player in the other team.

A player may place chips on either of the appropriate card spaces as long as it is not already covered by another marker chip.

If a player possesses a card which does not have an open space on the game board, the card is considered “dead” and may be exchanged for a new card. When it is your turn, click the dead card, and a "discard" button will appear under the card. Click it and the card will go to the discard pile. Then proceed to play your normal turn.

Strategy

Each corner of the board has a “Free” space that all players can use to their advantage. This space acts as if it has a chip of each color on it at all times - chips are never actually placed on this space.

To form rows, poker chips may be placed vertically, horizontally or diagonally. Each complete row of five (or four and a free corner space) is counted as a sequence. Sequences of the same color may intersect, but only at a single position.

Winning

The game ends when a player completes the winning amount of sequences: two sequences for two or four players (two teams); one sequence for three players (three teams).

Two Sequences: When two (2) sequences are required to win they can be in one straight row of ten chips. However this is accepted as a win only when one sequence does not interrupt the other. Once a sequence is made in the middle of the line it disrupts the ends. For example 5+5 is acceptable but a 2+5+3 is unacceptable.
